Domains Definition

Strategy

Competency Area Description
Corporate Strategy/Strategic Planning Finance teams are frequently tasked with the development and implementation of strategies to meet sustainable growth objectives. Strategic planning helps guide the deployment of resources so that the firm can create and sustain competitive advantages relative to its peers. The success of a given strategy is influenced by factors that are both internal (e.g., strengths and weaknesses) and external (e.g., opportunities and threats) to the firm. Finance teams can evaluate the relative success of the firm’s strategies by identifying and measuring key performance indicators (KPIs). In addition, balanced scorecards can be used to measure the firm’s progress in meeting strategic objectives
Data Strategy To become better business partners, finance teams must deliver more relevant and timely analyses and go beyond the siloed views of business. A key to this evolution resides in the mastering of data. Data is a key asset as finance teams continuously evaluate the firm’s strategies. To ensure reliable, transparent, and sustainable sources of data, finance teams must gain control of their data supply chain and ensure both an agile data acquisition process and that data is stored and organized properly. A well-designed data strategy will enable finance teams to lead with data by developing efficient and sustainable data-driven processes and in developing and implementing predictive business analytic capabilities

Gathering and Analyzing Information

Competency Area Description
Reporting and Analyzing Financial KPIs To provide business intelligence and actionable insights, finance teams must be able to gather, interpret, and analyze financial information. Gathering financial information consists of determining the financial information that is needed as well as the best source for the data. Interpreting and analyzing financial information requires knowledge of a wide variety of financial concepts (e.g., financial statements, ratios, data analysis, and balanced scorecards, among others). A broad knowledge base in these areas will allow finance teams to adequately monitor key performance indicators (KPIs) and make appropriate recommendations to upper management and other key influencers

Budgeting and Financal Principles

Competency Area Description
Budgeting Processes Budgets provide a tool to align the firm’s capital base with its mission and the needs of shareholders. For a budget to be effective, the goals of the budget must be clearly communicated to all areas of the organization. Effective budgets often utilize rolling forecasts to account for the dynamic nature of corporate operating environments. With rolling forecasts, the assumptions that are used to develop the budget are revised to reflect up-to-date information. During times of lower economic growth, a zero-based budgeting approach can be used to allocate capital to the areas of the firm that management feels are most strategic.
Understanding Financial concepts Finance teams are often tasked with analyzing and providing recommendations on capital investment opportunities. In addition to meeting strategic objectives, capital investments should be chosen to allocate capital to its highest and best use, which will ultimately maximize firm value. Subsequently, finance teams must be aware of the distinctions associated with different valuation approaches, such as discounted cash flow analysis, relative valuation models (i.e., multiples), and contingent claims analysis. In addition to evaluating capital investments, finance teams must often assess the overall risk of the enterprise and recommend strategies to hedge critical financial risks, including interest rate and foreign exchange rate risks.

Forecasting with Systems and Tools

Competency Area Description
Using Excel and Technology/Software Finance teams rely on technology and software to perform many aspects of their job roles. The dynamic and ubiquitous nature of Microsoft Excel’s worksheet tool makes it an essential element of financial modeling. Various worksheet functions improve the efficiency and effectiveness of financial modelers. Intermediate and advanced data analysis features of Excel allow the user to automate tasks and to provide deeper insights .
Financial Modeling Finance teams are frequently tasked with creating effective models that are accurate and dynamic. The latter refers to the user’s ability to change the value of model inputs to assess the impact on the model output. Two of the more common approaches used to assess the effect of model inputs on model outputs are sensitivity analysis (i.e., one model input is varied) and scenario analysis (i.e., where multiple model inputs are varied). Advanced approaches used to assess variability in financial model outputs include Monte Carlo Simulation and statistical analysis.

Communication and Presentation Techniques

Competency Area Description
Effective Communications Following the development of financial projections, finance teams must effectively communicate their results to the relevant stakeholders. One study estimates that 85 percent of success in business is determined by communication skills, and employers rank “verbal communication skills” as the most important skill necessary to do the job well. In addition to verbal communication, another key aspect of effective communication is to tailor the presentation materials and media to the audience, in an effort to ensure understanding, focus and fast actions. As a best practice, it is helpful for finance teams to develop a narrative that is portrayed by the financial projections. Essential elements of effective communications provide best practices in choosing the appropriate graphics for presentations and in writing reports.

Business Partnering

Competency Area Description
Influencing and Negotiating Over recent years, the roles of financial professionals have evolved significantly as the automation of basic tasks and the development of new analytical capabilities have provided additional time for focusing on high-level tasks. In its expanded role financial professionals help the business to align strategy with operations and in improve financial planning and budgeting. Consequently, financial professionals must think beyond their functional silos and partner and collaborate with other areas of the business. Further, since funding sources for budgets are finite, the ability to negotiate is a valuable skill for financial professionals. Negotiating skills will enable cost savings and lead to mutually-beneficial, long-term relationships with business partners.
Project Management Due to increasingly constrained resources, financial professionals are asked to step out of their firms’ traditional roles to serve as project managers. Project management involves balancing a series of simultaneous and sometimes overlapping project steps. Understanding and implementing project management best practices can lead to a more efficient and effective development process. A challenging aspect of project management is that the individuals involved may have different backgrounds and may work in different departments.
